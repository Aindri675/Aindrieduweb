About Me

My name is Ford Fox and I come from Tabubil, a vibrant town in the Western Province of Papua New Guinea. I was born on February 6, 2005, and have always been driven by a strong sense of purpose and faith. From an early age, I discovered that hard work and humility could open doors to great opportunities. My educational journey began in 2011 and I proudly completed Grade 12 in 2023. I am now an undergraduate student pursuing a Bachelor of Business and Management at IBS University. This academic path allows me to explore my interests in leadership, entrepreneurship, and community development. I consider education to be a powerful tool for transformation and social impact. Through my studies and extracurricular activities, I have cultivated skills in communication, planning, and teamwork. I am passionate about leading others with integrity, empathy, and vision. I enjoy organizing events that empower students and bring people together. My involvement in the 2024 Management Fest and Financial Literacy Workshop has helped me grow as a young leader. I am also creative and artistic—I love drawing, designing, and presenting ideas visually. My ambition is to one day lead my own business and use it to improve lives across Papua New Guinea. Outside of school, I enjoy reading motivational books, playing sports, and networking with like-minded youth. I believe that young people have the potential to drive change in society if given the right tools and mindset. Faith plays a central role in my life, and I always strive to put God first in all that I do. I value integrity, respect, and discipline as the foundation of personal and professional growth. I am committed to continuously learning, growing, and lifting others up along the way. My long-term goal is to become a leader who builds businesses that are ethical, inclusive, and impactful. I am determined to rise above challenges and leave a legacy that inspires others to do the same.
